Dissertation Research

"Hierarchical Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning for Quality-Aware Intelligent Distributed Manufacturing"
University of Louisville • In collaboration with IBM • NSF + IBM GOALI Funded

Level 1: Product-Level — Causal Defect Analytics

CF-MARL-SMV2D: A novel Counterfactual Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning framework for causal defect analysis in semiconductor manufacturing. Multiple collaborative agents explore the causal Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) space through adaptive soft-mask optimization and consensus mechanisms. Integrates a learnable domain-informed mask to encode expert knowledge and a counterfactual-based reward for robust causal identification.

Validation: Trained and evaluated on 5,173 real-world records (29 features) from a Fortune 500 computer-server manufacturer.

62.5%Reduction in Structural Hamming Distance
0.93True Positive Rate (TPR)
0.04False Discovery Rate (FDR)
96.1%Downstream defect prediction accuracy
8 SOTAmethods outperformed (NOTEARS, DAGMA, ALIAS)

LGRGANN + WBO-CIMP: A two-stage framework integrating GAN-augmented label reconstruction under incomplete annotations with cost-sensitive weighted Bayesian optimization. Designed for defect disposition under 20%+ missing labels and 5–10× asymmetric misclassification costs.

+21%Scrap-class F1 (0.76 → 0.92)
25.3%Reduction in expected cost
n=50Validation runs (10 seeds × 5 temporal folds)

Level 2: Factory-Level — Adaptive Production Scheduling

LHAC Framework: A Lexicographic Hierarchical Actor-Critic deep RL architecture for server-to-cell scheduling in configure-to-order manufacturing across 4 test banks × 14 cells. Agent 1 maximizes throughput while Agent 2 minimizes tardiness within Agent 1's near-optimal action set, enforcing strict objective prioritization without manual reward-weight tuning.

Novel Components: Deferred Action Placement for retry-based temporal flexibility, Critic-Augmented State Encoding for server-cell compatibility learning, and Feasibility-Preserving Reward shaping.

98.5%Completion rate (deterministic)
1.4%Tardiness rate
+53 ppvs. GA+CPLEX under disruption
20%Robust to cell disruptions

Hybrid RL+CPLEX Warm-Start: Pipeline that feeds trained LHAC policies as feasible initial solutions to a CPLEX MIP solver, recovering additional server placements on capacity-stressed instances where RL-only completion drops to 93%.

Level 3: System-Level — Distributed Manufacturing Coordination

HMARL Framework: A Hierarchical Multi-Agent RL framework for production management across distributed manufacturing sites. Higher-level agents coordinate global production targets and resource allocation while lower-level agents make local, real-time decisions for production rates and inventory replenishment.

Architecture: Centralized-training decentralized-execution (CTDE) paradigm with cooperative and competitive learning mechanisms for autonomous order offloading and inventory transshipment.

8.08%Cost reduction (within 0.44% of ILP)
8,340×Speedup (<0.01s vs. 83.4s)
500Orders scaled in <8s
87.5%On-time delivery at scale
