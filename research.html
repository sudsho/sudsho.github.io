Dissertation Research

"Hierarchical Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning for Quality-Aware Intelligent Distributed Manufacturing"
Advisor: Dr. Faisal Aqlan • University of Louisville • In collaboration with IBM

Level 1: Product-Level — Causal Defect Analytics

CF-MARL-SMV2D: A novel Counterfactual Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning framework for causal defect analysis in manufacturing. Multiple collaborative agents explore the causal Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) space through adaptive soft-mask optimization and consensus mechanisms. The framework integrates a learnable domain-informed mask to encode expert knowledge and a counterfactual-based reward for robust causal identification.

Key Results: Structural Hamming Distance reduced by 62.5% over best baseline. Downstream defect classification accuracy of 0.96. Low false discovery rate across all benchmarks.

Defect Disposition Prediction: A two-stage framework integrating label reconstruction under incomplete annotations (20% missing labels) with cost-sensitive weighted Bayesian optimization. Achieved 21% improvement in scrap detection F1-score and 25.3% reduction in expected misclassification costs.

Level 2: Factory-Level — Adaptive Production Scheduling

LHAC Framework: A Lexicographic Hierarchical Actor-Critic deep RL architecture for server-to-cell scheduling in configure-to-order manufacturing. Agent 1 maximizes throughput while Agent 2 minimizes tardiness within Agent 1's near-optimal action set, enforcing strict objective prioritization without manual reward-weight tuning.

Novel Components: Deferred Action Placement for retry-based temporal flexibility, Critic-Augmented State Encoding for server-cell compatibility learning, and Feasibility-Preserving Reward shaping.

Key Results: 98.5% completion rates. Outperforms optimization baselines by up to 53 percentage points under severe disruptions. Operates 60-75x faster than optimization-based methods. Robust under stochastic arrivals, processing noise, and equipment disruptions.

Level 3: System-Level — Distributed Manufacturing Coordination

HMARL Framework: A Hierarchical Multi-Agent RL framework for production management across distributed manufacturing sites. Higher-level agents coordinate global production targets and resource allocation while lower-level agents make local, real-time decisions for production rates and inventory replenishment.

Architecture: Centralized-training decentralized-execution (CTDE) paradigm with cooperative and competitive learning mechanisms for autonomous order offloading and inventory transshipment.

Key Results: Cost reductions within 0.5% of exact integer linear programming solutions while executing over 8,000x faster. Scales effectively to 500-order instances where exact optimization becomes intractable.
