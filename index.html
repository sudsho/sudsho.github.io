Research Focus

My dissertation, "Hierarchical Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning for Quality-Aware Intelligent Distributed Manufacturing," proposes a three-level AI framework validated on real-world data from a Fortune 500 computer-server manufacturer (IBM):

  • Level 1 — Product-Level Defect Analytics: Causal discovery via Counterfactual Multi-Agent RL (CF-MARL-SMV2D) on 5,173 records — reduced Structural Hamming Distance by 62.5%, achieved 0.93 TPR / 0.04 FDR / 96.1% accuracy, beating 8 SOTA methods. Plus LGRGANN + WBO-CIMP for cost-sensitive defect disposition under 20%+ missing labels.
  • Level 2 — Factory-Level Scheduling: LHAC, a lexicographic dual-agent deep RL scheduler — 98.5% completion / 1.4% tardiness, outperforming GA+CPLEX by up to 53 percentage points under disruption. Plus a Hybrid RL+CPLEX warm-start pipeline for capacity-stressed instances.
  • Level 3 — System-Level Coordination: HMARL, a hierarchical multi-agent RL system — 8.08% cost reduction within 0.44% of ILP, with <0.01s inference vs. 83.4s (8,340× speedup); scales to 500 orders in <8s.
